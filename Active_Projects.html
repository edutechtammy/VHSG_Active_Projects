Build Teams and Sprint Velocity

SCRUM Teams in TRELLO ...

Design Team should have the Storyboards and Scripts off to the Digital Art and Audio Team a month before their due dates.

Digital Arts Team should have their files off to the Dev team by the end of the day Friday before the lesson due date.

Audio Team should have narration files to Dev Team by the end of the day each Friday before the lesson due date.

Development Team should have their files off to the IT team by the end of the day each Saturday before the lesson due date.

IT Team should have all files uploaded by the end of the day Sunday of the due date.

The Design Team

The design team consistes of course project leads with a variety of responsibilities:

TRELLO Master


The Digital Arts Team

The Digital Arts team is the home of our visual artists. They make all the graphics and animation assets needed in the course page, lessons, games, simulations, and slides for the live online classroom.

TRELLO Digital Arts Team


The Audio Team

The Audio Team does the narration, music, and sound effects needed in lessons, simlulations, and games.

TRELLO Audio Team


The Development Team

The Development Team builds uses Captivate and does our raw coding for lessons, Study Notes, interactives, and more. The team members have the following skills:

TRELLO Dev Team


The IT Team

The IT team has access tot he server and will be responsible ofr getting needed files there as each team hands off files to the next team.

TRELLO IT Team


The Testing Team

Right now the testing team is within the Mission Possible task stream. This gives us the ability to test with a wide variety of devices among Mission agents. Later, we plan on training in a dedicated team for testing with its own workflows for course wide standards and for standards for each build type we do.

Sprint Velocity

These estimates do not include Moodle course page setup, question bank/questions, or assignment Sprint time.

Level 1, Basic: stock graphics, no audio or motion, no interactivity, abt. 15 slides/10 to 20 min lesson time

Level 2, Intermediate: stock graphics, narration, built-in animated transitions, some interactions such as drag and drop, abt. 15 slides/15 to 25 min. lesson time

Level 3, Advanced: extensive custom graphics/animations, simulations, complex practice and assessments, abt. 15 slides/25 to 30 minute lesson time

Work time is 40 hours per week.

Classes: All the same classes as the card to the left but with a style added in the div with the card class for height of the card to be set.

Styles - Sure, classes can handle styles, but then they are set in the css and not easy to apply on the fly in your HTML code. by having styles that you can apply, like height, you can set that information right where you are working with the content and layout. You can also set maximum height on and the card will not expand in height like the first card, instead a scrollbar in the card body will appear. This is actually in the class for all three examples, card-custom class, but it doesn't show until you set a tight heigtht and the content hits enough to hit that limit. The height is set with style="height: 450px" in the same div as the card class. Change the height and you will change when the scroll bar appears and the maximum height of the card.
